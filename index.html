About Me


Thomas Gauthier-Caron is a programmer interested in playful interaction. Thomas’ works defies the belief that computers are just zeros and ones. While it’s true that they are at their core, they become so much more when you introduce one key variable : people. Computers then become a way to play, to think, to form bonds with each other. This is why a lot of Thomas’ projects features not just human-computer interaction, but human-human interaction. Be it in a multiplayer screen based game, or a game of PONG played with seesaws. With a formation in both Computer Science and Computation Arts at Concordia University, Thomas is well positioned to craft powerful computer interactions that can surprise people and subvert expectations.

PARCADE

2015. Installation game. Personal work. Collaborative.

PARCADE is a transposition of the classic game PONG onto the cityscape. Instead of controlling the game with the traditional PONG controllers, players control the game with seesaws! Two seesaws are placed in front of a projection of the game and control the two paddles, the position of each paddle is mapped to the angle of the seesaws. The two­ player game is then transformed into a four­ player game, 2 vs 2. To win, players have no choice but to cooperate. This a collaborative project made with my brother, he designed the seesaws and did all the hardware. I programmed both the pong game and the microcontrollers in the seesaws.

Dataminer

2014. Web based game. Course work.

Dataminer is a protest game about you guessed it, data mining. The game is a take on Minecraft. Player log in with their Facebook account and mine blocks made up of the faces of other people who logged in. When blocks are mined, they give likes that the players can collect and use to craft personal information. The game shows real information from Facebook that players willingly provide by logging in. This project shows my ability to use APIs and use game design in a critical way.

Twitch Plays Tamagotchi

2015. Installation. Course work.

Twitch Plays Tamagotchi is a project Inspired from Twitch Plays Pokémon in which Twitch users can collaboratively control a Tamagotchi. It uses a real physical tamagotchi and not an emulator. I wanted originally to have little motors pressing the buttons, but I found a robotic finger that looked quite nice and I decided to use that instead. But when I received my Tamagotchi I realized the buttons were way too small so I used bigger buttons, whose inputs would be redirected to the Tamagotchi. The Twitch stream ran for a little more than a week and got over 50 000 views and was featured in multiple popular news websites.

PENTAGOD

2016. Game. Personal work.

PENTAGOD is a battle arena game inspired by dodgeball and featuring PONG physics. In this fantasy world inhabited by 2D shapes, the only being who is not a square, the Pentagod, organizes a battle to death among the poor square villagers of his kingdom. He takes great pleasure at watching the squares being slayed by a high velocity ball that gets thrown around in the arena. The only way to survive the battle is to perfect your jumping and aiming skills. Hit the ball towards you enemies when it's white, dodge it when it's not your color. May the winner live, hopefully. I did all the programming in this project, including player selection menus. This is a collaborative project.

Juncture

2017. Game. Ubisoft Game Lab Competition. Collaborative.

Juncture is a time-bending game about being friends and pulling off the ultimate heist. Two players try to inflitrate a manor. One player plays a first person stealth game while the other helps him around on a computer screen hacking doors, lasers and guards.
